Section 1

Executive Summary

Deal Insight is a pipeline intelligence overlay for Salesforce and HubSpot that tells revenue teams which deals are real, which are at risk, and where to spend their time. It sits on top of the existing sales stack — not as a replacement, but as the decision layer those tools were never built to be.

Know which deals are real. Fix the ones that aren't.

The product was born from the daily frustration of a senior enterprise sales leader with 30+ years of experience who built her own tools because the status quo wasn't cutting it. What started as a personal pipeline tracker evolved into a fully branded product with a working beta and a defined brand identity.

Core Features

Signals

Deal Insight's proprietary framework — six signals per deal: The Pain, The Value, The People, The Process, The Competition, The Momentum. Rep writes the story, AI scores the deal. One input, two outputs.

Deal Score

0–100 per deal, fed automatically by Signals. Answers: "Is this deal real?"

Deal Focus

Ranks deals by expected return on time. "These 3 deals deserve your attention today."

Deal Forecast

Confidence % per deal, rolled up for CROs. Replaces gut-feel forecasting with signal-based confidence.

Deal Rescue

Detects risk before the rep feels it. Surfaces playbooks with specific next actions. Saves one deal per quarter = pays for itself.

Section 2

The Problem

Your CRM said that deal was real. It lied.

The sales technology market is $80B+ and growing. Salesforce, HubSpot, and the rest are excellent at storing data. They are terrible at using it to make decisions.

For Reps

  • Sales platforms are data entry systems, not decision systems. 20–30% of time on fields that don't help close.
  • Pipeline reviews are theater. Stages updated to look good, not reflect reality.
  • No tool says where to spend time. Reps give equal attention to unequal deals.
  • Methodologies like MEDDPICC® exist in training decks, not in daily workflow.

For Managers & CROs

  • Forecasts based on rep optimism, not deal behavior.
  • Risk detection is reactive — by the time it's flagged, the deal is dead.
  • No coaching system — just 1:1 calls and gut instinct.

The Shadow CRM

Every high-performing rep has one — a spreadsheet, a notepad, a Trello board they actually use to manage deals. It exists because Salesforce and HubSpot are built for managers, sales ops, and rev ops — not for the frontline revenue generators who actually close the business. AEs, AMs, CSMs, BDs — the people bringing in the money — are stuck using tools designed for the people who report on the money.

Shadow CRMs aren't just an inefficient use of time and a duplication of effort — the real risk is that the information in them never makes it into the system of record. When the rep leaves, all of that deal intelligence walks out the door with them.

CRM Theater — updating fields that don't help you close, just to keep managers off your backs.

Reps spend 20–30% of their week on CRM Theater. The Shadow CRM is the proof that sellers need something different. Deal Insight is that something.

Optimism belongs in life. Not in your pipeline.

Section 4

Product Features

Signals — Deal Insight's Proprietary Framework

Signals unifies what the industry has always split into two things — the qualification scorecard (like MEDDPICC®) and the discovery narrative (like an Account Data Sheet). In practice, discovery IS qualification. They're the same conversation captured in two formats because someone decided one is for the rep and one is for the manager. Deal Insight doesn't have that split. It's one brain.

Write the story. We'll score the deal. Then we show you how to win it.

Six sections, covering everything:

1. The Pain — Why would they buy?

What's broken, what it's costing them, what they've tried. Merges SPIN®'s Problem/Implication, MEDDPICC®'s Identify Pain, Gap Selling's current state. AI scores whether pain is documented, quantified, and confirmed by more than one person.

2. The Value — Why us?

What they want to achieve, why your solution gets them there, and what that's worth in dollars. Merges MEDDPICC®'s Metrics, ValueSelling®'s entire premise. If a rep can't articulate the dollar value, the deal score drops.

3. The People — Who matters?

Stakeholder map — Economic Buyer, Champion, Blocker, Coach, End Users. Not just names and titles, but influence level and sentiment. AI flags if you only have one contact or no executive access.

4. The Process — How do they buy?

Who has the checkbook, does legal get involved, is there a board, how long does it take them to sign? Combines MEDDPICC®'s Decision Criteria + Decision Process + Paper Process into one narrative: "Here's how this company buys things."

5. The Competition — Why not someone else?

What alternatives are they looking at — another vendor, building it themselves, or doing nothing. Status quo is almost always the real competitor.

6. The Momentum — How do we get to the finish line?

Next steps, meeting cadence, timeline, close date movement. Not just "is this deal alive?" — this is the action section. Diagnose where the deal stands: is the next step concrete or vague, has the close date moved, when was the last meeting? Decide what needs to happen: re-engage the champion, introduce new stakeholders, deliver a value proof? Act on a specific play before the window closes. If the next step is vague, the close date has moved twice, and you haven't had a meeting in three weeks — the Deal Score tanks, and Momentum tells you exactly what to do about it.

Every section feeds the Deal Score automatically. Reps don't "fill in a checklist" — they capture what they know about the account, and Deal Insight extracts the signals and tells them where the gaps are. One input, two outputs: the rep gets their deal narrative, the manager gets their forecast confidence.

Evidence Scoring — The "How Do You Know?" Engine

Most CRM tools treat all data as equal — a rep typing "strong champion" is worth the same as a rep pasting an actual quote from the prospect. Deal Insight scores evidence quality on a hierarchy:

LevelEvidence TypeExampleConfidence
1Rep Assertion (weakest)"She spends a lot of time on manual reporting."Low
2Prospect Quote"I spend about 30% of my day pulling reports manually."Medium
3Quantified Consequence"If I had that 30% back, I could actually analyze the data — which is what I was hired to do."High
4Dollar Impact (strongest)"We lost probably $100K last year because we didn't realize we were close to a rebate tier."Highest

The AI doesn't just check "is Pain filled in?" — it reads what was written and scores the depth. A section full of Level 1 assertions gets a mediocre score. A section with prospect quotes and dollar impact gets a high score. And the AI coaching prompts the rep with the next question to ask:

"You've identified the pain but haven't quantified the cost. Try asking: 'What did that end up costing you last year?'"

The rep doesn't need to know they're running a SPIN implication chain — they just see "your Pain signal is at 60%, here's how to get it to 90%." Deal Insight does the methodology. The rep does the selling.

Deal Score (0–100)

A composite score answering "Is this deal real?" Fed automatically by Signals — calculated from pain documentation, value quantification, stakeholder coverage, process clarity, competitive position, and deal momentum. A deal with a low Reality Score but high ARR is a red flag, not a reason for excitement.

Deal Focus

Ranks all open deals by expected return on time invested. Output: "These 3 deals deserve your attention today." Solves the equal-time problem.

Deal Forecast

Confidence percentage per deal rolled up to portfolio level. A CRO sees: "We have $2.4M in pipeline, but only $1.6M at >70% confidence."

Deal Rescue

Detects risk before the rep feels it. Signals: stage age exceeding norms, meeting cadence drop, stakeholder gaps, email engagement decline, Signals gaps, close date pushed, historical loss patterns. Each alert comes with a Rescue Playbook — specific next actions.

Example: Risk = champion only. Playbook: (1) Ask who else needs to sign off. (2) Introduce value recap. (3) Request joint meeting with economic buyer.

Example: The Value Hail Mary. Deal is pushing? Stop asking for a "follow up." Send a 30-second video of the specific ROI gap you found in their SPIN data. Don't beg for time — deliver value that demands a response.

You said your deal is pushing? You mean your deal is dying.

Most tools stop at the alert. They never answer the only question that matters: now what? Deal Rescue does. Every alert comes with a specific play — the right move for the deal stage, the methodology, and the risk. The difference between losing a deal and saving one is almost always timing.

Section 5

AI-Powered Intelligence

Deal Insight isn't just a tracker — it's an AI-powered deal coach that works alongside the rep at every stage of the sales cycle. The AI layer touches pre-call, live-call, post-call, and ongoing pipeline management.

ICP Definition & Prospecting

AI helps define and refine the Ideal Customer Profile based on win/loss patterns, deal attributes, and firmographic data. Once the ICP is dialed in, AI identifies which prospects in the database best match — so reps stop wasting time on accounts that were never going to close.

Pre-Call Planning

Before every call, AI prepares the rep with intelligence on the industry vertical, the prospect company, and individual contacts. Reps can upload past calls, emails, and prior communications — AI synthesizes everything so the rep walks in informed and armed for discovery or demo. No more scrambling through old notes five minutes before a call.

Post-Call Analysis

After every call, AI produces:

  • Signals-based qualification — automatically scored and updated to define how the prospect buys
  • SPIN / Value Analysis — what we know, what we heard (in their words, with quotes), what they liked, what risks and blockers exist, what we don't know, and what to ask next
  • Call and deal summary — concise, shareable, ready for CRM or internal review

Sentiment & Next Steps

Based on call recordings and notes, AI objectively assesses customer sentiment and buying interest — not the rep's gut feeling, but data-driven analysis. From there, AI selects the appropriate playbook for next steps to move the deal forward, and assists with follow-up emails tailored to where the deal actually stands.

Objection Library

Three little words can start a love story. Five little words can kill a deal.

  • "Let me think about it"
  • "Send me some more info"
  • "We don't need anything new"
  • "Call me in six months"
  • "We can't afford it now"

AI-powered objection handling built from real deal context. When a prospect pushes back, Deal Insight doesn't leave the rep scrambling — it serves up the right response based on the objection type, the deal stage, and the methodology the team runs. Format: The prospect said X → You say/do Y.

Examples:

  • "We need to think about it" → Isolate the real concern: "Totally understand. When you say think about it, is it the timing, the investment, or something we haven't addressed yet?"
  • "It's too expensive" → Reframe to value: "Compared to what? Let's look at what one rescued deal per quarter is worth to your team."
  • "We're happy with what we have" → Challenge the status quo: "Most teams are — until they see what they're missing. Can I show you what pipeline blind spots look like?"
  • "Send me some info" → Redirect to action: "Happy to — but the most useful thing I can send is specific to your situation. Can I ask two quick questions so I don't waste your time with a generic deck?"

The library grows over time as AI learns from what actually works across the team's deals — which responses led to next meetings, which killed momentum. Reps get smarter objection handling without memorizing scripts.

Proposal & Collateral Generation

AI produces branded proposals with pricing, relevant customer case studies, competitor comparisons, and a Mutual Action Plan — all generated from the deal context. No more copy-pasting from old decks at midnight before a deadline.

Rep Performance Scoring

AI objectively scores rep performance on sales proficiency based on call analysis — covering AE, AM, Pre-Sales, and CS interactions. This creates a training opportunity with specific suggestions for improvement. Scores can be sent privately to the rep for self-learning, or shared with leadership and sales enablement for guided coaching.

Automated CRM Sync

Reps never have to manually upload information into the system of record. All call notes, Signals scores, deal summaries, and activity data are seamlessly pushed to the CRM for leadership visibility. The rep focuses on selling. The CRM stays clean. Everyone wins.

Ongoing Pipeline Temperature Monitoring

AI continuously monitors the full pipeline to direct reps on how to best use their time: which deals are real, which are going off track, when and how to move a deal forward, how to fix deals that can still be won, and how to cut bait on the ones that feel good but are a pipe dream wasting your time.

AI proactively issues alerts when critical milestones are in danger of being missed and deals are slipping — before the rep even realizes it.

The Philosophy

AI is doing a few things at once: monitoring the pipeline FOR the rep so they can focus on additional revenue generation, and providing an objective view of their pipeline that cuts through the optimism bias every seller carries. It's not replacing the rep — it's giving them superpowers.

Stop updating. Start prospecting. The AI handles the rest.

Account Photos

Attach a photo to any account — a screenshot from a Gong call, a LinkedIn headshot, a whiteboard from a meeting. Put a face to the deal. Pipeline stops being rows in a spreadsheet and starts being people you're actually selling to.

Keep deals human.

Gong Transcript Integration

Pull call transcripts from Gong automatically and feed them directly to the AI. Video is heavy — storage, streaming, encoding, permissions. But a Gong transcript is structured text, and structured text is exactly what AI processes best. Transcripts feed the Signals scorer, the Objection Library, sentiment analysis, and next-step recommendations. The transcript is actually more useful than the video for Deal Insight's purposes because the AI can parse it instantly — every call becomes intelligence without the rep lifting a finger.

Methodology-Agnostic, Methodology-Aware

Deal Insight doesn't force one methodology — it's methodology-agnostic but methodology-aware. The AI looks at the deal stage, what's been covered, what's missing, the prospect's vertical, their history, and generates the right questions and talking points. A rep running MEDDPICC® gets MEDDPICC®-aligned prompts. A team that uses Challenger gets commercial teaching scripts. A BDR doing cold outreach gets SPIN®-style discovery questions.

Your deal playbook — written FOR you.

Color Usage Hierarchy

The four brand colors are not four equal accents. They form a system: one primary, three signal.

Primary Brand Color: Teal

Teal is the product color. Use for UI highlights, links, buttons, active states, logo accents. Teal reads as data, intelligence, signal. It is the interface. It stays clean and constant.

Signal Colors (the Signals system)

Amber, Pink, and Purple become meaning-based, not decorative. Each one carries a specific signal inside the product:

  • Amber Attention. Emerging issue. Something needs a look.
  • Pink Opportunity. Movement. Momentum shifting.
  • Purple Critical insight. High-impact deal signal.

This structure does two things. Investors immediately see a system, not random colors. Product features align naturally with the palette. When a Signal fires inside the product, it carries a color. That color means something.

Teal = product. Amber / Pink / Purple = signals
Signal colors never decorate. They always communicate

Themes and Signal Separation

Two color layers run in parallel inside the product. They never cross.

Layer 1: Signals (untouchable)

Amber, Pink, Purple signal indicators on deal cards, badges, and left-border markers are sacred. They mean the same thing for every user, every theme. No customization. No override. This is the product speaking.

Layer 2: Chrome (personalizable)

Header bar, sidebar, primary action buttons. This is the mood layer. The rep picks their vibe:

  • Zen Chrome goes teal. Calm, focused, steady.
  • Bold Chrome goes pink. Aggressive, high-energy closer.
  • Chill Chrome goes purple. Cool, methodical, strategic.
  • Warm Chrome goes amber. Relationship builder, consultative.

Light themes use Nurture-intensity shades for chrome. Dark themes use Hot-intensity shades. Signal dots stay at full intensity regardless of theme.

Chrome says "this is my workspace." Signals say "this deal needs attention"

Legal Review — Pre-Launch Items

Sales Methodology Trademarks

Deal Insight references established sales methodologies throughout its product and marketing. Several of these are registered trademarks that require careful handling before launch.

Known Registered Trademarks

  • MEDDPICC® — federally registered, owned by Darius Lahoutifard / MEDDIC Academy (registered 2021). Actively enforced.
  • SPIN® / SPIN Selling® — registered, owned by Huthwaite International (federal registrations since 1988).
  • Challenger Sale — owned by Challenger Inc (spun out of CEB/Gartner). Trademarked in education/training context.
  • ValueSelling Framework® — registered, owned by ValueSelling Associates, LLC.
  • Sandler Selling System® — registered, owned by Sandler Systems Inc.
  • Solution Selling® — was trademarked; enforcement status unclear after multiple acquisitions (now Korn Ferry).
  • BANT — not trademarked. Generic industry acronym (IBM, 1960s). Safe to use freely.
  • Command of the Message® — trademarked by Force Management.
  • Gap Selling — not formally trademarked to current knowledge. Worth verifying.
  • Conceptual Selling® — registered, owned by Korn Ferry (Miller Heiman).
  • New Sales. Simplified. — book title (copyrighted) by Mike Weinberg. Not a registered trademark for training services. More referencing flexibility than ® marks.

What We Can Do (Nominative Fair Use)

We can reference methodology names to describe what Deal Insight is compatible with or supports. Nominative fair use allows using another's mark to identify that party's product or service when it can't be readily identified otherwise — as long as we don't imply endorsement or affiliation. Similar to saying "works with Salesforce" on a product page.

Examples of acceptable language:

  • "Supports MEDDPICC®-based qualification scoring"
  • "Works alongside SPIN Selling® discovery frameworks"
  • "Draws on proven methodologies including Challenger Sale® and Value Selling®"

Required Safeguards

  • Use the ® symbol when referencing registered marks
  • Include attribution footnotes in marketing materials and product UI, for example:
    "MEDDPICC® is a registered trademark of Darius Lahoutifard / MEDDIC Academy. SPIN Selling® is a registered trademark of Huthwaite International. ValueSelling Framework® is a registered trademark of ValueSelling Associates, LLC. Sandler Selling System® is a registered trademark of Sandler Systems Inc. Conceptual Selling® is a registered trademark of Korn Ferry. Command of the Message® is a registered trademark of Force Management. Deal Insight is not affiliated with or endorsed by any of these organizations."
  • Never imply endorsement or affiliation with any methodology owner
  • Never use their marks in Deal Insight feature names (e.g., don't call it "the MEDDPICC Module")
  • Name our features with Deal Insight's own language — "Deal Score" not "MEDDPICC Score," "Discovery Framework" not "SPIN Questions"

Risk Area

MEDDIC Academy's stated position is that unauthorized use of the MEDDPICC name or methodology can result in legal action. The underlying concepts (qualifying deals by identifying an economic buyer, understanding decision criteria, etc.) are general sales practices and not ownable. But the specific branded acronym and any proprietary training content are protected.

Recommended Approach

Use methodology names referentially in marketing and UI (with ® and attribution), but name all Deal Insight features with our own language. In descriptions, say things like "inspired by proven methodologies like MEDDPICC® and SPIN Selling®" rather than claiming direct implementation.

Action Item: Engage a trademark attorney for a pre-launch review. A quick consult would confirm safe usage and give peace of mind. Estimated cost: a few hundred dollars. Specific asks: (1) confirm nominative fair use of third-party methodology names, (2) trademark search on "Signals" as a deal qualification framework name — the word is generic in prospecting/intent-data contexts but no one appears to own it for deal qualification or scoring.

Signals Framework — IP Distinctiveness

Signals is Deal Insight's proprietary framework. It is legally distinct from MEDDPICC® and other methodologies for several reasons:

  • Different structure — MEDDPICC is 8 letters, each a binary checklist item. Signals uses 6 narrative sections with different groupings (e.g., Decision Criteria + Decision Process + Paper Process are combined into one section, "The Process").
  • Different purpose — MEDDPICC is a qualification checklist (yes/no gaps). Signals is a narrative framework where the rep tells the deal's story and AI extracts the score. Fundamentally different product experience.
  • Different name, different brand — No acronym, no overlap in terminology.
  • Concepts are universal — "Who's the decision maker?" and "what's the competition?" are foundational sales concepts appearing in virtually every methodology since the 1960s. Salesforce, HubSpot, Clari, and Gong all have fields for "champion," "economic buyer," and "competition" without infringing.
  • Includes elements not in MEDDPICC — The Momentum section (diagnose-decide-act framework for next steps, cadence, close date movement) doesn't exist in MEDDPICC at all.

Recommendation: don't position it as "our version of MEDDPICC" — position it as its own thing (which it genuinely is). Confirm with trademark attorney at pre-launch.

Other Pre-Launch Legal Items

  • Domain ownership confirmation (dealinsight.io via Namecheap)
  • Terms of Service and Privacy Policy drafting
  • Confirm "Deal Insight" / "DealInsight" trademark availability and file if clear
  • Data handling compliance (if integrating with Salesforce/HubSpot customer data)
  • SOC 2 Type II certification — required for enterprise SaaS handling customer pipeline data
  • GDPR compliance — required for any EU customers or data subjects
  • WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility compliance — ensure the product is usable for visually impaired and other disabled users. Not optional, not a nice-to-have. Build it accessible from day one.
